Why Planning Your IKEA PAX Wardrobe Matters

An IKEA PAX wardrobe is one of the most popular storage solutions in UK homes. Versatile, affordable, and customisable, the PAX system can transform a bedroom — but only if you plan it correctly. Rush the layout and you could end up with awkward gaps, mismatched internal fittings, or a wardrobe that simply doesn't work for your space.

That's why using an IKEA PAX planner UK tool before you buy a single frame is so important. Whether you're measuring a tricky alcove, working around a sloped ceiling, or trying to squeeze in a double wardrobe alongside a bed, having a visual plan saves time, money, and frustration.

In this guide, we'll walk you through everything you need to know about planning a PAX wardrobe — from taking accurate measurements to using free online floor planning tools to see how your wardrobe fits within the wider bedroom layout.

Understanding the IKEA PAX System

Before diving into planning tools, it helps to understand how the PAX system works. PAX wardrobes are modular, meaning they're made up of individual frame units that slot together. You choose the frames, add doors, and then fill the inside with shelves, drawers, rails, and organisers from the KOMPLEMENT range.

Standard PAX Frame Sizes

In the UK, PAX frames come in a range of standard widths and heights:

  • Widths: 50 cm, 75 cm, and 100 cm
  • Heights: 201 cm and 236 cm
  • Depth: 35 cm or 58 cm

By combining different frame widths, you can create a wardrobe that fits almost any wall. For example, two 100 cm frames plus one 75 cm frame gives you a 275 cm run — useful for many standard UK bedroom walls.

Why Precise Measurements Are Essential

Unlike fitted wardrobes, PAX units don't trim down to size. You need to work with the standard dimensions and find a combination that fits your available space. A gap of even a few centimetres between the wardrobe and the wall can look untidy or prevent doors from opening properly.

This is where digital planning tools become invaluable. Instead of sketching on graph paper and hoping for the best, you can map out your bedroom accurately and experiment with different frame combinations before spending a penny.

Using the IKEA PAX Planner UK Tool

IKEA offers its own wardrobe planner on its UK website, which lets you configure frames, doors, and internal fittings digitally. It's a useful starting point, particularly for visualising how different door styles and interior configurations look together.

However, the IKEA PAX planner UK tool focuses specifically on the wardrobe itself — it doesn't show you how the wardrobe fits into your actual bedroom. For that, you need a full room planner.

What the IKEA Planner Does Well

  • Lets you mix and match frame sizes to hit a target width
  • Shows door style options (sliding, hinged, mirrored)
  • Helps you visualise internal KOMPLEMENT configurations
  • Generates a shopping list automatically

Where It Falls Short

  • Doesn't show the rest of your bedroom layout
  • Can't account for windows, doors, radiators, or sockets
  • Doesn't help you check clearance space for swing doors
  • No option to plan adjacent furniture like beds or dressing tables

This is exactly why using a dedicated room planning tool alongside the IKEA planner gives you a much more complete picture.

Planning Your Full Bedroom Layout First

Before you finalise any wardrobe configuration, it's worth stepping back and planning your entire bedroom layout. This helps you answer questions like:

  • Which wall is the best fit for the PAX run?
  • Will hinged doors have enough clearance to open fully?
  • Is there enough space between the wardrobe and the bed to move comfortably?
  • Can you fit a dressing table or chest of drawers alongside?

A free, browser-based floor planner like the one at freeroomplanner.com lets you draw your bedroom to scale and experiment with different furniture arrangements — all without signing up, downloading anything, or entering your email address.

The bedroom planner tool on the site uses a snap-to-grid system (snapping to every 10 cm) so your measurements stay accurate. You can draw walls, add doors and windows, and then drag and resize furniture items to find the best layout. Live measurements display in metres, centimetres, or feet, making it easy to cross-reference with IKEA's standard frame sizes.

Step-by-Step: How to Plan Your PAX Wardrobe Using a Free Room Planner

  1. Measure your bedroom accurately. Use a tape measure to record the length and width of the room, plus the position of windows, doors, and any architectural features like alcoves or chimney breasts. Note the height of your ceiling too.
  2. Draw your room to scale. Head to freeroomplanner.com and use the bedroom planner to draw your walls. The snap-to-grid system makes this straightforward — click to place walls and the tool keeps everything proportional.
  3. Mark out windows, doors, and radiators. These are the obstacles that will influence where your wardrobe can go. Add them to your floor plan so you can see the available wall space at a glance.
  4. Experiment with wardrobe placement. Use the furniture tool to drag a wardrobe-shaped rectangle onto the wall where you're thinking of placing your PAX units. Resize it to match the total width of your planned configuration. Check that there's enough clearance for doors to open and that walkways around the bed aren't too narrow.
  5. Check standard clearances. For hinged PAX doors, you'll need at least 58 cm of clear space in front of the wardrobe (matching the door width). Sliding doors need no clearance in front but require an overlap between panels. Make sure your floor plan reflects this.
  6. Finalise your frame combination. Once you know the maximum width available, calculate which combination of PAX frame sizes fits best. Remember, frames come in 50 cm, 75 cm, and 100 cm widths. Aim to fill the space as closely as possible, leaving a small gap (no more than 5 cm) that can be filled with a filler strip or plinth.
  7. Take your plan to IKEA. Export your floor plan as a PNG from freeroomplanner.com and bring it with you to IKEA or share it with a bedroom fitter. It gives everyone a clear reference point and reduces the chance of ordering the wrong items.

Tips for Specific Bedroom Scenarios

PAX in an Alcove

Alcoves are popular spots for built-in-style PAX wardrobes, but they come with challenges. The alcove may not be perfectly square, and the width might not match standard PAX sizes exactly. Use your room planner to map the alcove precisely, then work out which frame combination comes closest to fitting. Consider leaving a small gap on one side and using a filler strip for a neater finish. If the alcove is deeper than 58 cm, you may also want to consider a front panel or pelmet to close the gap above the cornice.

Floor-to-Ceiling PAX

In rooms with high ceilings, some homeowners extend their PAX wardrobes to the ceiling using a topper unit. This creates a built-in look and maximises storage. If this is your goal, measure your ceiling height carefully and check that IKEA's topper panel dimensions work with your chosen frame height.

PAX as a Room Divider

In open-plan spaces or large bedrooms, PAX units are sometimes used as room dividers — for example, to separate a sleeping area from a home office. If you're planning something like this, the multi-room floor plan maker at freeroomplanner.com can help you visualise the full space and make sure the divider works ergonomically and aesthetically.

Fitted Wardrobe Look on a Budget

Many homeowners use PAX as the base for a more fitted wardrobe look by adding custom doors, panels, and cornices. If you're working with a carpenter or joiner to achieve this effect, having an accurate floor plan to share is essential. Export your bedroom plan from freeroomplanner.com and use it as the basis for your brief.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is there a free IKEA PAX planner for UK customers?

Yes. IKEA's own wardrobe planner is available on the IKEA UK website and lets you configure frame sizes, door styles, and internal fittings. However, it only shows the wardrobe itself — not how it fits into your room. For a complete bedroom layout, use a free room planner like freeroomplanner.com alongside the IKEA tool.

What is the maximum width of a PAX wardrobe combination?

There's no fixed maximum, as you can combine as many frames as your wall space allows. However, IKEA recommends securing frames to the wall for stability, and very wide combinations may need additional support. In practice, most UK bedrooms accommodate combinations between 150 cm and 300 cm wide.

Do I need to create an account to use freeroomplanner.com?
