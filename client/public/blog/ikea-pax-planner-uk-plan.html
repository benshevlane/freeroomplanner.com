Planning Your IKEA PAX Wardrobe in the UK: Everything You Need to Know

If you've ever stood in the IKEA bedroom section surrounded by flat-pack boxes and wondered whether any of it will actually fit your room, you're not alone. Planning a PAX wardrobe system is one of the most common home improvement challenges UK homeowners face — and getting it wrong can be an expensive mistake.

The good news is that there are free tools available to help you visualise your space before you spend a single penny. Whether you're using the official IKEA PAX planner UK tool or looking for a more flexible floor-planning alternative, this guide will walk you through everything you need to plan confidently, avoid common pitfalls, and end up with a wardrobe setup that genuinely works for your bedroom.

What Is the IKEA PAX System?

The PAX wardrobe is IKEA's modular storage system that has become a staple in British bedrooms. The system is built around a range of freestanding wardrobe frames in standard sizes, which you then fill with internal fittings like shelves, drawers, rails, and organisers from the KOMPLEMENT range.

Standard PAX Frame Sizes Available in the UK

PAX frames come in a limited set of standardised dimensions, which makes planning much easier once you know them:

  • Width: 50cm, 75cm, or 100cm
  • Depth: 35cm or 58cm
  • Height: 201cm or 236cm

By combining these frames side by side, you can create a built-in look that spans an entire wall. The most popular UK configurations tend to use 2–4 frames together, sometimes finished with panels or plinths to give a fully fitted appearance.

Using the IKEA PAX Planner UK Tool

IKEA offers its own online PAX planner on the UK website, which is specifically designed to help you configure wardrobe combinations. Here's how it generally works:

  1. Choose your frame size: Select width, height, and depth based on your room's measurements.
  2. Pick your doors: Hinged or sliding, in a range of finishes.
  3. Add internal fittings: Drawers, shelves, trouser hangers, shoe racks, and more.
  4. Review the shopping list: The planner generates a parts list with prices.
  5. Save or share: You can save your design to your IKEA account.

The IKEA PAX planner UK tool is genuinely useful for configuring the interior of your wardrobe. However, it does have a key limitation: it doesn't show you how the wardrobe will sit within your actual room. That's where a dedicated room planner becomes invaluable.

Why You Should Plan Your Whole Bedroom First

Many homeowners make the mistake of designing their PAX wardrobe in isolation, only to find that once it's assembled, it blocks a window, impedes a door swing, or simply overwhelms the room. Before you dive into wardrobe configuration, it pays to map out your entire bedroom to scale.

Key Questions to Answer Before Ordering

  • How much wall space do you actually have available?
  • Does the wardrobe placement affect natural light?
  • Will doors — including wardrobe doors — open without hitting each other or furniture?
  • Is there enough circulation space around the bed and other furniture?
  • Are there sockets, radiators, or light switches that could be affected?

These are exactly the kinds of issues a proper floor plan helps you catch before it's too late.

PAX Wardrobe Planning Tips for UK Bedrooms

Working with Sloped Ceilings

Many UK homes — particularly loft conversions and rooms in older properties — have sloped or angled ceilings. Standard PAX frames at 236cm may not fit under a slope. In these cases, the 201cm height option is usually the better choice, or you may need custom solutions above the frames.

Fitting PAX into Alcoves

Alcoves beside chimney breasts are common in Victorian and Edwardian terraced houses across the UK. PAX frames can work well here, but you'll need to account for any skirting boards, coving, or irregular wall angles. Measure the alcove width carefully — if it doesn't match standard PAX widths exactly, you may need to add a filler panel or opt for a bespoke solution.

Considering the Depth

The 58cm deep PAX frame is the standard wardrobe depth and accommodates most hanging clothes comfortably. The 35cm option is shallower and works well for folded items, shoes, or in rooms where space is tight. Be sure to account for door clearance — sliding door PAX systems don't need the same swing clearance as hinged options, which can be a significant advantage in smaller UK bedrooms.

PAX as a Room Divider

In larger bedrooms or open-plan spaces, a back-to-back PAX configuration can act as a room divider, creating a dressing area or separating a home office nook. Alternatives to the IKEA PAX System

While PAX is the most popular modular wardrobe in the UK, it's worth knowing your alternatives — particularly if your room has unusual dimensions:

  • Hammonds, Sharps, and Spaceslide: UK-based fitted wardrobe specialists who can work with non-standard wall widths.
  • Neville Johnson: Premium fitted furniture for more design-led projects.
  • B&Q and Wickes: Own-brand modular wardrobe systems at lower price points.
  • Local carpenters: Bespoke built-in wardrobes that make full use of awkward spaces.

Whatever route you choose, the principle is the same: measure carefully, plan your layout first, and use a floor plan to communicate your vision clearly.

FAQ: IKEA PAX Planner UK

Can I use a room planner instead of the IKEA PAX planner?

The two tools serve different purposes. The IKEA PAX planner UK tool is designed to configure the internals of your wardrobe — drawers, shelves, and fittings — and generate a shopping list. A room planner like FreeRoomPlanner.com is designed to show you how the wardrobe fits within your actual room. Using both together gives you the most complete picture.

What are the standard PAX wardrobe depths and will they fit in a small UK bedroom?

PAX frames come in 35cm and 58cm depths. In a smaller UK bedroom — particularly those under 3 metres wide — the 35cm option can be a useful space-saver. Always model the wardrobe in a floor plan first to check circulation space around other furniture, especially the bed.

Can I plan a full fitted wardrobe wall using a free room planner?

Yes. With FreeRoomPlanner.com, you can draw your bedroom to scale and place furniture items at any dimension, allowing you to model a full PAX wall accurately. The live measurement display shows you exact dimensions as you work, making it easy to confirm the wardrobe span matches your wall.

Is the IKEA PAX system suitable for sloped ceilings common in UK loft bedrooms?

Standard PAX frames are rectangular and available at two fixed heights (201cm and 236cm). They don't automatically adapt to sloped ceilings. In a loft conversion, you may be limited to the shorter height option, or you may need additional carpentry work above the frames. Planning your loft bedroom carefully using a room planner can help you visualise these constraints before purchasing.
