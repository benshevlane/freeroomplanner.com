Common Floor Plan Errors a Good Tool Prevents

Most renovation mistakes don't happen during the build. They happen during the planning — or the lack of it. Here are five errors that come up repeatedly, and how snap-to-grid accuracy catches them early.

  1. Ignoring door swing clearance. A door that opens into a narrow hallway or swings into a radiator is a problem that only shows up once it's fitted. Drawing doors to scale — with their arc — flags the conflict immediately.
  2. Forgetting radiator positions. Radiators eat wall space and restrict furniture placement. Mark them on your floor plan from the start, not as an afterthought.
  3. Underestimating walkway width. Kitchen guidance typically suggests a minimum of 90 cm between parallel runs of cabinets for comfortable single-person use, and around 120 cm for two people. Live measurements make it easy to check this before cabinets are ordered.
  4. Placing furniture without checking sightlines. A TV positioned opposite a window creates glare. A sofa with its back to the door feels uncomfortable. A 2D floor plan shows these relationships clearly.
  5. Scaling from memory. People consistently misjudge room size. The gap between what a room feels like and what it actually measures is where most furniture-buying regret originates. A snap-to-grid floor plan anchors every decision in real numbers.

A 2D floor plan shows your room from above — walls, furniture, doors, and dimensions laid out flat. A 3D room planner adds a rendered view from inside the space. Most homeowners find 2D plans faster to create and easier to share.
